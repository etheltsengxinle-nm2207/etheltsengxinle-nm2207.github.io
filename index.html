Step 1: Problem

We need to find out the frequency of borrowed words in Chinese to cultivate a greater appreciation for the corss-cultural interactions in language.

CURRENT RESEARCH

Shen (2009) examines the types of words borrowed from English in Chinese language, proposing that such words can be categorised into certain domains such as politics, economics, arts and entertainment, religion etc.

Explore specific borrowed words in English and Chinese!

WHY THIS PROBLEM?

Recent research has explored the types of words that are borrowed in different languages, yet not enough research is done to generate a data visualisation of the frequency of usage regarding certain words.

Discover the importance of borrowed words in language!

OUR AIM

This study aims to fill in the gaps of data visualisation in the field of linguistic study, providing a more nuanced understanding of data and the claims made in current linguistic research.

Read more below!

STEP 2: RESEARCH QUESTION

How can we track and make sense of the frequency of borrowed words from English to Chinese in the 20th/ 21st century? (Are the trends similar/ different/ unrelated/ interdependent? Do certain groups of words have a stronger relation to each other than other groups of words?)
